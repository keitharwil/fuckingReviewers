> Important Ways to Classify Information

> Classification by Characteristics
  • Strategic: Long-term policy decisions(e.g., new product).
  • Tactical: Control over resources(e.g., budgeting, quality control).
  • Operational: Day-to-day specific tasks(e.g., shift report).
> Classification by Application
  • Planning: Establishing norms and specifications
  • Control: Monitoring activities via feedback
  • "Information about information," acquired through experience.

> Classification by Application
  • Organizational Information
  • Deals with an organization's environment and culture in light of its objectives, crucial for uncertainty reduction.
  • Function/Operational Information
  • Operation-specific details, such as daily schedules or duty rosters, mostly internal to the organization.
  • Database Information
  • Large quantities of information stored, retrieved, and managed for multiple usages and applications
