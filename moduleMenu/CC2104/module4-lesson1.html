favorite Lesson 1. Overview of Information Systems

  • An information system (IS) is an interconnected set of components used to collect, store, process and transmit data and digital information.
  • An IS comprises an integrated set of hardware and software, plus databases and communications networks that facilitate data collection, storage and use.
  • People and processes are also vital components of an IS.

> Who uses information systems?

  • Information systems are crucial drivers of the modern digital economy.
  • A reliable IS enables different departments, teams and functions to access and share the data they need for their specific purposes.
  • For example, the accounting team may use an IS to streamline audit processes, identify and manage financial risk, and generate accurate financial reports.
  • Other business functions that typically use ISes within their operations include:
    • Human resource (HR) management
    • Operations
    • Supply chain management
    • Customer support
    • Information security and cybersecurity
    • Risk management
    • Compliance

  • Input: First the system collects data as input. This data can come from typing, voice commands, touch screens, and sensors. The input data can be structured (organized) and unstructured (disorganized).
  • Processing: Since the input data is raw, the computer's processor (CPU) must organize it into a structured format. It does this through steps like sorting, grouping, searching, analyzing, and making reports.
  • Storage: Next, the organized data is stored temporarily or permanently. It gets stored in databases, hard drives, or solid-state drives.
  • Output: The stored data is then analyzed and presented in a useful way like reports, dashboards, or data visualizations.
  • Feedback: Finally, the system gets feedback from users on their experience. This helps measure how well the system is working.
