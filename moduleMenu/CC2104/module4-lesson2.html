favorite Lesson 2. Types of Information Systems

  • The effectiveness of any enterprise IS depends on whether it aligns with the organization's information requirements, as well as its business goals, usability and features related to information security and regulatory compliance.

> Organizations may implement different types of IS, such as the following:

  • Transaction Processing System (TPS)
    • These process transactions and produce reports. It refers to the automation of basic, repetitive processing that underpins business operations.
  • Management Information System (MIS)
    • This is a well-known information system to organize the information, which processes data and converts it into meaningful information.
    • MIS is capable of providing analysis, planning & decision developing support. Marketing, manufacturing, human resources, finance, and accounting are some of the functional areas of a company.
  • Decision Support System (DSS)
    • This is an information system a formal request to be considered for a position or to be allowed to do or have something.
    • DSS be inclined with planning, analyzing alternatives, and trial and error search for the solution.
    • DSS processes information to support the decision creating process of a manager.
  • Executive Support System (ESS)
    • This is a reference of the management information system, which is a special kind of DSS; An ESS is specially tailored for the ownership of the chief executive of an association.
  • Office Automation Systems (OAS)
    • This is referenced to the application of computers and communication technology to office functions.
    • Office activities may be grouped under two classes, namely:
      • Activities executed by clerical staff like clerks, secretaries, typist etc.
      • Activities performed by the executives (managers, engineers or other experienced like economist, researchers etc.)
    • In the first category, the following is a list of activities:
      • Typing
      • Mailing
      • Scheduling of meetings and conferences
      • Calendar keeping
      • Retrieving documents
    • In the second category, the following is a list of activities:
      • Conferencing
      • Production of information (messages, memos, reports, etc.) and controlling performance

> Other types of IS include the following:

  • Knowledge Work System (KWS)
    • These systems provide useful insights for knowledge workers, such as researchers, analysts and consultants.
  • Knowledge Management System (KMS)
    • A KMS is an information system used to update and manage the organization's knowledge resources.
  • Inventory Management System (IMS)
    • An IMS collects inventory information and generates insights to help with inventory management.
  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform
    • A CRM platform is a comprehensive tool or set of tools that enables organizations to view customer information in real time to guide their sales, marketing, support, commerce and other decisions.
