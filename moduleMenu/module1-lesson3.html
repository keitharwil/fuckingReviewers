> Data vs. Information vs. Knowledge

  • While often used interchangeably, data, information, and knowledge represent distinct stages in the transformation of raw facts into actionable insights.

Data --> Information --> Knowledge --> Wisdom --> Business Knowledge

> Information vs Data

  • Data can be described as unprocessed facts and figures.
  • Plain collected data as raw facts cannot help in decision-making.
  • Information is interpreted data; created from organized, structured, and processed data in a particular context.
    • Data --> Processing --> Information
    Process of Transforming Data into information

> Information Hierarchy by Professor Ray R. Larson:
  • Data - raw material of information
  • Information - data organized and presented
  • Knowledge - information read, heard, seen or understood
  • Wisdom - Distilled and integrated knowledge
> Information Continuum by Scott Andrews:
  • Data - a fact or piece of information
  • Information - Knowledge discerned from data
  • Business Intelligence - organization's policy or decision-making

> Information/Data Collection Techniques
  • Surveys
  • Structured questionnaires designed to collect data directly from a target
  • Secondary Data Sources
  • Leveraging existing data from old records
  • Objective Measures/Tests
  • Data through controlled experimental tests conducted on a subject or system.
  • Interviews
  • collection of responses to pre-defined questions through personal discussions with individuals.
