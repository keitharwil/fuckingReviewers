> Types of Information

‣ Factual Information
  • Objective data that can be verified as true or false. Free from bias, it forms the bedrock of knowledge.
‣ Subjective Information
  • Personal opinions, interpretations, feelings, or beliefs.
  • Inherently biased, it provides diverse viewpoints.
‣ Analytical Information
  • Processed and analyzed data
  • Adds interpretation and context to make data meaningful.
‣ Procedural Information
  • Step-by-step instructions or guidelines for tasks.
  • Practical and action-oriented, crucial for correct execution.
