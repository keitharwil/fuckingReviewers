Quality Information

  • High-quality information is a cornerstone of organizational success, enabling proactive decision-making and identifying opportunities and problems in advance.
  • Accessible: Easily obtainable by authorized users in the right format and at the right time.
  • Accurate: Error-free, avoiding "garbage in, garbage out" scenarios resulting from inaccurate data.
  • Complete: Contains all important facts; for example, an investment report must include all costs.
  • Economical: Relatively inexpensive to produce, balancing value against cost.
  • Flexible: Adaptable for various purposes, changing needs, and environmental conditions.
  • Relevant: Important and pertinent to the decision-maker's current needs.
  • Reliable: Trustworthy, verifiable, and dependable, often linked to data collection methods
  • Secure: Protected from unauthorized access by users.
  • Simple: Not overly complex; detailed information may not always be needed.
  • Timely: Delivered when needed; outdated information has little value.
  • Verifiable: Can be checked against multiple sources to ensure correctness.
