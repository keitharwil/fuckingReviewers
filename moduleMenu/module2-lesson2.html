> Data Management vs Information Management

  • Information management is an organizational program that manages the people, processes and technology
  • Provides control over the structure, processing, delivery and usage of information required for management and business intelligence purposes

  • Data management is a subset of information management.
  • It comprises all disciplines related to managing data as a valuable, organizational resource.
  • It's the process of creating, obtaining, transforming, sharing, protecting, documenting and preserving data.

> Differences of Information Management and Data Management

>>Information Management<<
  • Organizational program that manages the people, processes and technology
  • Includes both electronic and physical information
  • Has many formats including data, paper documents, electronic documents, audio, video, etc.
>>Data Management<<
  • Ensures data that underlies an organization is available, accurate, complete and secure.
  • All disciplines related to managing data as a valuable, organizational resource
  • Can take many different forms, including numbers, letters, sets of characters, images, etc.
